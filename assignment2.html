In this circuit, I used 1 blue LED. To find the resistance, I used Ohm's Law: Voltage(Volts) = Current(Amperes) x Resistance (Ohms)

The voltage in this formula is the power of my laptop, which is 5V.

First, I need to find the voltage of the resistor:

V(total) = V(blue LED) + V(resistor)

<=> 5 = 3.3+ V(resistor) => V(resistor) = 5-3.3 = 1.7V

Apply it to the formula, V = I x R

<=> 1.7 = 0.02 x R => R(resistance) = 1.7/0.02 = 85 Ohms

Because we don't have 85 Ohms resistance in the kit, so I used the 100 Ohms instead, and it's closest to 85!

For the button, I chose 10k resistor to control the current.
